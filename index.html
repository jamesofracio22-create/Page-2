What Is Bullying

Bullying is unwanted, aggressive behavior among school-aged children that involves a real or perceived power imbalance. The behavior is repeated or has the potential to be repeated over time. Both kids who are bullied and those who bully others may have serious, lasting problems.

We can help prevent bullying by becoming an "upstander"--a person who takes action when they see bullying--and by fostering a culture of kindness and respect.

Types of Bullying

HOMEPAGE TYPES OF BULLYING PREVENTION

1. Physical Bullying: Hitting, kicking, shoving, spitting, stealing, or damaging property.

2. Verbal Bullying: Name-calling, mocking, teasing, insults, slurs, or sexual harassment.

3. Social Bullying: Excluding others, gossiping, spreading rumors, or damaging reputations.

4. Racial Bullying: Treating people badly because of their racial or ethnic background, calling racist names, or telling racist jokes.

5. Disability Bullying: Targeting someone because of a disability, making them uncomfortable, or using hurtful jokes.

6. Cyber Bullying: Using email, texts, social media, or online platforms to threaten, harass, embarrass, or exclude others.
